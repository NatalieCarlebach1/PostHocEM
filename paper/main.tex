\begin{abstract}
Semi-supervised learning (SSL) methods for medical image segmentation discard the unlabeled data at convergence, even though the model's predictions on it still contain residual uncertainty concentrated at organ boundaries.
We show this residual entropy is spatially structured and exploit it with \textbf{Post-hoc Entropy Minimization (PEM)}: any converged SSL checkpoint, fine-tuned for $2$--$5$ epochs with a single entropy loss on unlabeled data --- no labels, no pseudo-labels, no architectural changes.
Applied to the publicly released BCP~\cite{bai2023bcp} checkpoints with no test-set tuning, PEM improves Dice by $+1.12$ / $+1.43$ / $+0.64$ on Pancreas-CT 20\%, LA 5\%, LA 10\% and reduces HD95 by $27$--$43\%$, in under $5.2$\,min per run on a single GPU.
Against three controlled post-hoc baselines (temperature scaling, pseudo-label fine-tune, self-consistency), PEM attains the largest mean gain on the two less-saturated configurations.
\end{abstract}

\begin{keywords}
Semi-supervised learning, medical image segmentation, entropy minimization, post-hoc fine-tuning
\end{keywords}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Recent SSL methods for 3D medical image segmentation~\cite{tarvainen2017meanteacher,yu2019uamt,bai2023bcp,liu2024most,assefa2025dycon} discard the unlabeled data once training converges.
We make a complementary observation.
Measuring the per-voxel softmax distribution of the publicly released BCP checkpoints on the unlabeled training set, we find that \textbf{$89$--$95\%$ of their total prediction entropy is concentrated in a thin uncertain-voxel shell ($5$--$9\%$ of the volume), whereas a supervised-only baseline trained on the same labels carries only $\sim$$55\%$ of its entropy in the corresponding shell} (Tab.~\ref{tab:entropy}).
This $34$--$40$\,percentage-point gap is a property of SSL convergence and is the precondition under which a post-hoc, label-free refinement step is well-conditioned.
We exploit it with \textbf{Post-hoc Entropy Minimization (PEM)}: take a converged SSL checkpoint and fine-tune it for $2$--$5$ epochs with a single entropy loss, optionally restricted to a confidence shell.
Entropy minimization itself is classical~\cite{grandvalet2004entropy,sohn2020fixmatch}; our contribution is its \emph{operating regime} --- applied to a converged optimum whose residual entropy is already structurally concentrated, the gradient signal is automatically aligned with the locus of error, so the same loss needs no masking, curriculum, or class-balance regularizer.
Under a fixed-budget protocol with no test-set tuning, PEM improves Dice by $+0.6$ to $+1.4$ and HD95 by $27$--$43\%$ across three BCP configurations; all three gains pass a per-case Wilcoxon signed-rank test at $p\!<\!0.025$.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\noindent\textbf{Shell entropy concentration.}
For a checkpoint $f_{\theta^*}$, unlabeled volume $\mathbf{x}\in\mathcal{U}$, per-voxel softmax $p_i$, per-voxel entropy $H_i=-\sum_c p_{i,c}\log p_{i,c}$, and confidence $c_i=\max_c p_{i,c}$, define
\begin{equation}
    \rho_f(\tau) \;=\; \tfrac{\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}\sim\mathcal{U}}[\sum_{i} H_i\,\mathbb{1}[c_i\le\tau]]}{\mathbb{E}_{\mathbf{x}\sim\mathcal{U}}[\sum_{i} H_i]} \in [0,1],
    \qquad
    \mathcal{L}_{\text{PEM}} = \tfrac{1}{|\mathcal{U}|}\!\sum_{\mathbf{x}}\tfrac{\sum_{i} m_i H_i}{\sum_i m_i+\epsilon},\; m_i=\mathbb{1}[c_i\!\le\!\tau].
    \label{eq:rho}
\end{equation}
$\rho_f(\tau)$ is the fraction of total prediction entropy carried by voxels of confidence at most $\tau$; it is label-free, self-localizing (on a converged network, low-confidence voxels lie near decision boundaries), and scalar. We use $\tau\!=\!0.99$ throughout, with $\rho^\star\!=\!0.85$ as a suitability threshold.
Tab.~\ref{tab:entropy} shows the three BCP checkpoints concentrate $\rho_f(0.99)\!\in\!\{0.894,0.948,0.945\}$ while a supervised-only VNet on the same $12$ Pancreas-CT labels attains only $0.552$; uncertain voxels in the latter lie on average $14.5$ voxels from any decision boundary (vs.\ $5$--$11$ for SSL).
For a binary voxel with logit $z$ and probability $p\!=\!\sigma(z)$, $\partial H/\partial z\!=\!-z\,p(1\!-\!p)$: the update vanishes as $p\!\to\!0$ or $1$ and peaks at $p\!=\!0.5$, so the $p(1\!-\!p)$ factor is an implicit boundary-shell mask equivalent to $m_i$, and no class-balance regularizer is needed. Because the feature-space decision surface co-locates with the organ surface in 3D segmentation, PEM implements the cluster assumption~\cite{chapelle2006ssl,grandvalet2004entropy} at the organ surface; when residual entropy is diffuse ($\rho_f\!\lesssim\!0.6$) sharpening reinforces existing errors.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\noindent\textbf{Setup.} We fine-tune the released BCP~\cite{bai2023bcp} checkpoints on Pancreas-CT~\cite{roth2015pancreas} (VNet-IN, $12$/$50$/$18$) and LA 2018 (VNet-BN, $4$ or $8$/rest/$20$) with BCP's canonical preprocessing. Adam (no WD, no schedule), grad-clip $1$, LRs $5{\times}10^{-5}$/$1{\times}10^{-5}$ (Pancreas/LA) chosen via $5$-fold CV on labeled folds; LA BN stats frozen. Epoch budget fixed \emph{a priori} at $E{=}2$/$5$; PEM rows are the mean over seeds $\{2020,42,123\}$. Sliding-window inference with BCP's original strides, no TTA; runs take $\leq\!5.2$\,min on a single RTX $4070$\,Ti SUPER. We compare against BCP because MOST~\cite{liu2024most} and DyCON~\cite{assefa2025dycon} release training code only; code at \url{<anonymized>}.

\noindent\textbf{Post-hoc baselines.} Same BCP checkpoint, same budget, same LR/data as PEM:
\textbf{TS} optimizes a scalar $T$ on unlabeled entropy (cannot alter argmax);
\textbf{PL-FT} generates hard pseudo-labels (argmax$+$largest CC) and fine-tunes with CE$+$Dice;
\textbf{SC} minimizes MSE between student-on-noised and frozen-teacher-on-clean forwards.

\noindent\textbf{Results (Tab.~\ref{tab:main}).}
TS produces $|\Delta\mathrm{Dice}|{\le}0.03$ on all settings, so PEM's gain must come from parameter updates at boundary voxels rather than recalibration.
PEM attains the largest Dice and HD95 improvement on the two less-saturated configurations ($+1.12$/$+1.43$ vs.\ $+0.50$/$+1.09$ for PL-FT); on the saturated LA 10\% (BCP $89.40$) PL-FT marginally beats PEM ($+0.80$ vs.\ $+0.64$) with overlapping bootstrap CIs, consistent with the soft $p(1{-}p)$ gradient shrinking as $\rho_f\!\to\!1$.
Three-seed PEM ($84.01{\pm}0.00$/$88.59{\pm}0.25$/$90.02{\pm}0.07$) and per-case paired analysis ($N{=}18{-}20$, Wilcoxon $p{<}0.025$, CI lower bound $\geq{+}0.18$, $d_z{\in}[0.57,0.71]$, $13$--$16$ wins) confirm the gain on all three settings.

\noindent\textbf{Ablations (LA 5\%, varying one knob).} Tightening $\tau$ from $1.0$ to $0.95$ raises the gain $+0.51\!\to\!+1.39$ Dice; $\tau\!=\!0.999$ discards the shell, $\tau\!=\!0.90$ admits saturated noise. LR window is narrow: $<\!10^{-6}$ triggers collapse protection within one epoch, $>\!5{\times}10^{-5}$ relocates rather than refines the boundary. Dice is monotonic in $E$ up to a peak at $E\!=\!8$ ($+1.61$); we report the a-priori $E\!=\!5$.

\section{Discussion and Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

\textbf{(C1)} The three BCP checkpoints satisfy $\rho_f(0.99)\!\in\![0.894,0.948]$ while a supervised-only VNet on the same labels attains $0.552$ --- a $34$--$40$\,pp gap that is the precondition for PEM. \textbf{(C2)} Whenever (C1) holds, post-hoc entropy minimization without labels, pseudo-labels, or test-set tuning improves Dice by $+0.6$ to $+1.4$ and HD95 by $27$--$43\%$ in under $5.2$\,min per run. PEM fails outside this regime in two ways ruled out by a single label-free forward pass: diffuse-entropy collapse ($\rho_f\!\lesssim\!0.6$, observed on the supervised baseline) and vanishing-entropy diminishing returns ($\rho_f\!\to\!1$, where PL-FT becomes competitive --- LA 10\%).
